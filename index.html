Michael Jackson was One of the greatest Singer and dancer of all times. Lets have a loOk At his Journey !
  • 29th August 1958-Michael Jackson was born.
  • 1962- Michael starts singing lead vocals in a local band.
  • 1968 Motown signs Michael Jackson
  • 1970- "I want you back",“ABC,” “The Love You Save” and “I’ll Be There” also become Number-One single By Michael jackson
  • 1972- Michael releases his first solo album "Got To be There" whose 5.2 Million copies are sold worldwide
  • 1978- 1978: Jackson makes his acting debut as the Scarecrow in The Wiz, an urban retelling of The Wizard Of Oz.
  • 1980-Jackson wins his first Grammy Award for Best R&B Male Vocal Performance.
  • 1982-1983-Jackson releases Thriller, which tops the charts for 37 weeks. Seven of the albums nine songs became Top 10 hits.During the extended video feature, directed by John Landis, Jackson unveiled his signature dance move,

    the MOONWALK

  • 1987- Jackson’s releases his seventh album, Bad, behind which he embarks on a relentless world tour
  • 1988-Jackson stars in the film Moonwalker, a collection of short films about Jackson.
  • 1990-the Guinness Book of World Records certifies the record as the best-selling album ever. To date, it has sold over 65 million copies.
  • 1990-Jackson releases another album, Dangerous. Dangerous was Jackson’s second album to debut at #1 on the Billboard 200 – a spot it retained for four weeks. It has sold 32 Million copies worldwide and a Grammy for Best Engineered Album.
  • 1997-Jackson is inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame.
  • 2001-Sony releases Michael Jackson’s tenth and final studio album, Invincible, which was well-received by contemporary music critics.
  • 2008- 2008: Jackson issues Thriller 25, celebrating 25 years of the iconic album. The reissue hits number one in eight countries and reached number two in the U.S. Sony releases King of Pop, a fan-curated compilation.
  • June 25, 2009-Jackson passes away as a result of cardiac arrest in Los Angeles at the age of 50.

On the 52nd Annual GRAMMY Awards in 2010, GRAMMY winners Smokey Robinson, Celine Dion, Carrie Underwood, Usher, and Jennifer Hudson teamed for an all-star 3-D rendition of "Earth Song" in tribute to Jackson, who that year was honored posthumously with a Recording Academy Lifetime Achievement Award.
